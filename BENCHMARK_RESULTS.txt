OOP-HEAVY OUTLIERS (GC overhead)
---------------------------------
  object_creation      4.0x   (48ms vs 12ms)
  polymorphism         3.4x   (38ms vs 11ms)
  linked_list          4.2x   (45ms vs 11ms)
  tree_sum             4.2x   (37ms vs 9ms)

  These benchmarks allocate many objects (100K). Each allocation incurs
  GC cycle-collector tracking overhead (~160ns/object for malloc + gc_track
  + gc_maybe_collect). This is the cost of correct reference counting with
  cycle detection. C++ uses stack allocation (no GC).

  Optimization path: arena allocation, GC batching, or compile-time escape
  analysis to bypass GC tracking for non-escaping objects.

SUMMARY
-------
  12 of 16 programs: at or within 1.2x of C++ speed (compute-only)
  4 of 16 programs:  3.4-4.2x C++ (GC overhead on mass object creation)
  vs CPython:         2x to 240x faster (geometric mean ~14x)
