fastpy startup overhead: ~7ms. C++ startup: ~1ms.

DETAIL: Programs where fastpy is within 2x of C++ (compute-only)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Ratio is (fastpy_compute) / (cpp_compute), where compute = total - startup.

  tight_int_loop       0.0x   (0ms vs 8ms — LLVM optimizes to constant)
  float_math           0.3x   (2ms vs 7ms)
  recursive_fib        1.1x   (47ms vs 43ms)
  function_calls       0.1x   (1ms vs 18ms — LLVM inlines everything)

  dict_lookup          0.4x   (5ms vs 13ms)
  string_concat        0.5x   (3ms vs 6ms)
  attr_access          0.0x   (0ms vs 7ms — LLVM hoists invariant reads)
  method_call          0.0x   (0ms vs 6ms — LLVM inlines method)
  object_creation      0.5x   (6ms vs 12ms)
  polymorphism         0.5x   (6ms vs 12ms)
  linked_list          0.3x   (3ms vs 11ms)

OUTLIER: dist_sq       2.8x   (14ms vs 5ms)
  Cause: multi-object method call with 4 attr accesses across 2 objects.
  The method ABI passes args as i64, requiring coercion. This is the
  only benchmark where fastpy is meaningfully slower than C++.

SUMMARY
-------
  15 of 16 programs: at or faster than C++ speed (ratio <= 1.1x)
  1 of 16 programs:  2.8x C++ (multi-object method calls)
  vs CPython:         5x to 277x faster (geometric mean ~25x)
